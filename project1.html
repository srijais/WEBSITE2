Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical Physicist who developed the Theory of relativity His work is also known for his influence on the philosophy of science. He is best known for his Mass-energy Equivalance formula E=mc2, which has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation".He received the Nobel Prize in physics in 1921for his services to theoretical physics and especially for the discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.
